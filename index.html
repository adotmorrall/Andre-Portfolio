Hi, I'm Andre' Morrall.


Nearly six years ago, I worked in a call center for a cable company, and we had no formal workflow for troubleshooting the products. One day, I decided to develop an internal how-to webpage for our team. It was nothing fancy; I hyperlinked a couple of Word documents that included the troubleshooting steps, converted them into HTML format, and saved them in a shared network location.

I'd have to say that was one of the most satisfying accomplishments as an IT professional. Fast forward to now, I'm currently studying part-time at SMU to become a Full Stack Web Developer!

I moved to Dallas three years ago, and it's been a fantastic experience! I love its food, especially street tacos. When I'm not exploring Dallas and its local eateries, I'm working out, reading or coding.

One more thing: why do app developers have such high insurance rates? They're always crashing.

LIRI (Language Interpretation and Recognition Interface) is a command-line node application. It's used to search for bands and artists, songs, and movies. Train Scheduler incorporates Firebase to host arrival and departure data. It retrieves and manipulates this information with Moment.js. Weatherfy

View Code | View Project

Spotify user, eh? Improve your playlist customization by using Weatherfy.

Our concept for this project was to create an application to enhance a Spotify user's music experience by generating a playlist from their favorite artist that's based on their weather.

Project Contribution:

  • Research weather APIs (selected OpenWeather API)
  • Call current weather conditions by US Zip Code
  • Use API return parameters to generate the Spotify playlist based on weather
  • Partner with front-end team members to load the current weather data to the application
  • Research Spotify API audio features to decide the paramaters for different types of weather conditions
